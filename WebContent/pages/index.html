About Ephemera Project

Chinese Commercial Advertisement Archive (CCAA) is an image-oriented and research-directed online archive that opens to all scholarly communities. The archive consists of high-quality digital images of advertisements for international brands published in leading Chinese newspapers from the late 19th century to the middle of 20th century in China, along with research metadata for each image. All advertisements for all commodities that foreign companies produced and marketed are included. We selected the major commercial newspapers in each of the five most significant Chinese treaty port cities. These include Shanghai’s Shen-pao (申报), Tianjin’s Ta Kung Pao (大公报), Shenyang’s Sheng-ching Shih-pao (盛京时报), Hankou’s Hankow Times (汉口中西报) and the Yuet Wa Po (越华报) in Guangzhou to insure the sample is representative and comparative.

Given the milieu of global capitalism in the colonial period, the CCAA’s theoretical objective is to create a means of drawing general pictures of social-economic change in major Chinese treaty-ports and their marketing catchment areas, between the late 19th and mid-20th centuries. We hope to better understand how people in the new environments thought about commodity culture. In this regard, the CCAA’s first set of goals included: 1) collecting and digitizing advertisements collected from the five representative newspapers; 2) designing and laying the foundations for a searchable, online Archive; and 3) placing all images in the Archive, fully coded, free for use and publicly accessible on the Web.
